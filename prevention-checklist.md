# Stay Safe Online  
## A Checklist Every College Student Should Follow  

---

## General Online Safety  

### 1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords  
Don't use your name, birthday, or '1234' as passwords. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use different passwords for different apps — if one account gets hacked, the others stay safe.  

### 2. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks  
Free Wi-Fi at cafes, college canteens, or railway stations is risky. Hackers can spy on your activity. Never log in to banking apps, UPI, or email on public Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data instead.  

### 3. Keep Your Apps and Phone Updated  
App updates often fix security issues. If you ignore updates, your phone becomes easier to hack. Turn on auto-updates so you don't have to think about it.  

### 4. Think Before You Click Any Link  
If someone sends you a link on WhatsApp, Instagram, or email saying "You won a prize" or "Your account will be blocked", stop and think. These are usually scams. Do not click or share such links.  

---

## Social Media and Privacy  

### 5. Never Share OTP, PIN, or Password With Anyone  
No bank, app, or government officer will ask for your OTP or PIN. If someone calls asking for it, it is a scam. Do not share it.  

### 6. Be Careful What You Post Online  
Photos showing your college ID, tickets, Aadhaar card, or home address can be misused. Once something is online, it is difficult to remove. Think before posting.  

---

## UPI and Financial Safety  

### 7. PIN is for Sending Money, Not Receiving  
Scammers may send a collect request and tell you to enter your PIN to receive money. This is false. Entering your PIN will send money from your account.  

### 8. Verify Before Paying Any Seller Online  
When buying from unknown sellers or websites, verify them first. Check reviews and avoid paying full amount in advance to someone you do not know.  

### 9. Enable Transaction Alerts and Set UPI Limits  
Turn on SMS and app notifications for every transaction. Set a daily limit to reduce loss in case of fraud.
